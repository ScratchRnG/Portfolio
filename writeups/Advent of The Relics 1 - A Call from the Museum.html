Overview

In this Sherlock-style investigation, an employee at CALE receives an unexpected email and follows its instructions, triggering suspicious activity that causes them to panic and pull the power cable. A month later, the same email becomes the starting point for a cyber investigation. The objective is to trace the infection chain starting from the email, identify the malicious attachment and indicators, and uncover the command-and-control infrastructure and second-stage delivery.

Key Artifacts

  • Email content and header metadata
  • Malicious ZIP attachment delivered via email
  • LNK artifact pointing to PowerShell execution
  • PowerShell script behavior and outbound network activity

Approach & Techniques Used

  • Email & Header Analysis: validated the suspicious sender versus legitimate mail relay infrastructure and traced delivery paths.
  • Attachment & Artifact Review: identified the ZIP payload and extracted key indicators from its contents.
  • LNK & PowerShell Investigation: analyzed shortcut execution leading to PowerShell-based activity.
  • Network & C2 Analysis: identified outbound POST requests and second-stage infrastructure contacted by the script.

Conclusion

This investigation highlights how a single phishing email can serve as the entry point for a multi-stage attack. By combining email triage, artifact analysis, and PowerShell execution tracing, it becomes possible to reconstruct the attacker’s activity and identify command-and-control infrastructure. This challenge reinforces the importance of strong email analysis skills and disciplined investigation workflows in real-world incident response scenarios.
